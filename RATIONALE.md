# Nomination rationale — KANSL3 → MLL1-WDR5 chromatin-modifying assembly (CORUM 5386)

**Why it was flagged (pipeline-internal, DepMap-blind).** KANSL3 carries a conformally certified AlphaFold-Multimer edge to complex member KANSL1 (conformal risk p=0.0066 < q=0.10) plus a second certified edge to HCFC2 (p=0.0055), a paralog of the complex member HCFC1. These survive distribution-free FDR control, so they are not benchmark-prevalence artifacts.

**Why it is biologically real (held-out referee + literature).** The independent DepMap co-essentiality referee — which never enters the structure score or the labels — places KANSL3 tightly with the NSL sub-module of this CORUM entry: KANSL1 (GLS p=2.3e-20), MCRS1 (p=1.2e-06) and PHF20 (p=6.4e-03), all positive-sign co-essential. KANSL1, KANSL2 and KANSL3 are the scaffolding subunits of the NSL (KAT8/MOF) histone-acetyltransferase complex (Dias et al., Genes Dev 2014, PMC4018492; Chatterjee et al., Mol Cell 2021, PMC8056186), whose canonical composition is KAT8, KANSL1/2/3, MCRS1, PHF20, WDR5, OGT and HCFC1 — precisely the proteins KANSL3 scores against here.

**Why it appears as a 'missing' member of *this* complex.** WDR5 is a shared hub: its interactions with the MOF/NSL complex and with the MLL/COMPASS methyltransferase complex are mutually exclusive (Dias et al. 2014). CORUM 5386 ('MLL1-WDR5 complex') therefore captures WDR5 and several NSL proteins (HCFC1, KAT8, MCRS1, PHF20) but omits KANSL3. An AlphaFold-Multimer screen, scoring pairwise interfaces, naturally recovers the KANSL3–KANSL1 (and KANSL3–HCFC2) contacts and nominates KANSL3 as the missing scaffold.

**Independent structural corroboration.** A Boltz-2 prediction — a model architecturally independent of the AlphaFold-Multimer that produced the audited edge — recovers a localized KANSL3–KANSL1 interface (global ipTM 0.47; a compact, confidently-placed contact patch KANSL3[279–448]–KANSL1[624–690], 90 inter-chain contacts, interface pLDDT 0.70/0.59, best-contact PAE 4.8 Å). Global confidence is moderate, depressed by the large disordered regions of both proteins; the signal is a specific docked segment rather than a solved whole-complex fold. This is honest corroboration of the certified edge from a second structure model, not a claim of a high-confidence complex.

**Independent confirmation (held out of the pipeline).** KANSL3 is an annotated member of the NSL complex in CORUM's own database (complex 7221, PMID 20018852) but is absent from the target entry 5386 — a database-internal positive control the audit did not use.

**Cancer relevance (honest scope).** KANSL3 is NOT a Cancer Gene Census driver (COSMIC): this is an essentiality-based, not mutation-based, nomination. The NSL/KAT8 axis is a validated cancer dependency — the NSL complex is required for cell survival (Chatterjee et al. 2021), KAT8 is an active anticancer target (PMC12154333), and KANSL3-derived HCF1-competitor peptides are anti-proliferative across cancer cell lines (bioRxiv 2026.03.13.711608). The target complex is anchored by the leukemia oncoprotein KMT2A(MLL1) and by WDR5, both established drug targets in AML/MLL-rearranged leukemia.

**Falsifiable prediction.** If KANSL3 is a true member of the WDR5-shared assembly, CRISPR knockout of KANSL3 should phenocopy KANSL1/MCRS1/PHF20 loss in the same DepMap lines, and a KANSL3–KANSL1 interface should co-purify in WDR5/KAT8 pulldowns from MLL-rearranged leukemia cells.
